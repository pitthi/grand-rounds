Health Informatics Grand Rounds

University of Pittsburgh

School of Health and Rehabilitation Sciences

Department of Health Informatics

Organized by the Department of Health Informatics, School of Health and Rehabilitation Sciences at the University of Pittsburgh, the Health Informatics Grand Rounds is a publicly and freely available seminar/webinar series which mainly aims to offer a professional forum for health data scientists and AI-powered healthcare researchers to present their latest research findings and innovations.

We aim to expose students to cutting-edge work in health informatics and to engage faculties to build new collaborations by inviting world-class external speakers from both academia and industry. The Health Informatics Grand Rounds is sponsored by the Center for Bioethics and Health Law, the Pitt Research, Ethics and Society Initiative, and the IEEE Computer Society at Pittsburgh.

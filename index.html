Organized by the Department of Health Informatics, School of Health and Rehabilitation Sciences, and the School of Computing and Information at the University of Pittsburgh, the Health Informatics Grand Rounds offers a "monthly-basis" publicly and freely available seminar/webinar series which mainly aims to provide a professional forum for health data scientists and AI-powered healthcare researchers to present and share their latest research findings and innovations.

We aim to expose students and individual researchers to cutting-edge research and innovation in health informatics, engaging faculties to build new collaborations by inviting world-class external speakers from both academia and industry. The Health Informatics Grand Rounds is sponsored by the Center for Bioethics and Health Law, the Pitt Research, Ethics and Society Initiative, and the IEEE Computer Society at Pittsburgh.


Key Points

  • Health Informatics Grand Rounds is a publicly and freely available seminar/webinar series
  • It is a monthly-basis seminar/webinar series. One-hour scientific talk
  • The talks will be recorded, and the recoding(s) will be sent to those who register for the talk
  • If you would like to present at the Health Informatics Grand Rounds, or have a question, please contact us
